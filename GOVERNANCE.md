# Governance

Umbral exists because open-source infrastructure must remain community-governed.

## Stewardship

[Moonshot Ideas](https://github.com/moonshotideas) is the current steward of the Umbral project. We are committed to transferring governance to an independent foundation once the project reaches 1.0 stability.

Until that transition, Moonshot Ideas funds development, maintains infrastructure, and ensures the project stays aligned with its mission: providing a fast, reliable, open-source Python package manager that belongs to the community.

## Decision-Making

### Day-to-Day

Maintainers review and merge pull requests. Any maintainer can approve and merge a PR that passes CI and has no unresolved objections. If two maintainers disagree, a third maintainer breaks the tie.

### Major Changes (RFCs)

Changes that affect public APIs, add new crates, modify the lockfile format, or alter the project's scope require an RFC:

1. Open an issue with the `rfc` label describing the proposed change, motivation, and alternatives considered.
2. Allow at least 7 days for community discussion.
3. Maintainers vote to accept or reject. A simple majority is required, with a minimum of 3 votes.

### Maintainer Voting

For merge decisions where consensus cannot be reached informally, maintainers may call a vote. Each maintainer gets one vote. Simple majority wins. Votes are recorded in the relevant issue or PR.

## Maintainers

### Becoming a Maintainer

Any contributor with 3 or more merged pull requests may be nominated for maintainership. The process:

1. An existing maintainer opens a nomination issue.
2. The nominee accepts the nomination.
3. After a 7-day discussion period, existing maintainers vote. A two-thirds majority is required.
4. If accepted, the new maintainer is granted write access and added to the maintainers list.

### Maintainer Responsibilities

- Review pull requests in a timely manner.
- Participate in RFC discussions and votes.
- Uphold the Code of Conduct.
- Act in the best interest of the Umbral community.

### Stepping Down

Maintainers may step down at any time by notifying the other maintainers. Maintainers who are inactive for 6 months may be moved to emeritus status after a discussion.

## Code of Conduct

All participants in the Umbral project are expected to follow our [Code of Conduct](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for everyone.

Reports of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be sent to opensource@moonshotideas.com.

## Foundation Transition

At 1.0, governance will transfer to an independent foundation. The foundation charter will be drafted as an RFC and ratified by maintainer vote before the transition. The goal is to ensure Umbral's long-term independence from any single company.
